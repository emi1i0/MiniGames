# Cannon Dodge — Art Direction: "Sunlit Cove"

The feeling to chase: a bright, sunny pirate cove seen from a gull's-eye view —
warm, playful and inviting, so the tension comes entirely from the black balls
tearing across it, never from a gloomy palette. Think illustrated arcade key art
(bold shapes, clean outlines), not photoreal.

## Principles

1. **The island is warmth; the threat is cold and black.** The sand and sea are
   saturated, sunlit and friendly. The only truly dark, high-contrast element on
   screen is the cannonball — a matte black sphere with a single specular dot and
   a faint ground shadow. That contrast is the whole game: your eye must snap to
   the balls instantly against any sand tone, so nothing else on the sand is
   allowed to be that dark or that round-and-black.

2. **Readability beats decoration.** Palms, rocks, foam and sand speckle exist to
   give the cove life, but they live near the shore and stay low-contrast. The
   centre is a deliberately lighter "clearing" so the busiest play space is the
   calmest to read. If a decoration ever competes with a ball for attention, it is
   wrong — dim it or move it out.

3. **Every threat is telegraphed in warm-to-danger.** A cannon about to fire draws
   a dashed line that charges from soft amber to red, plus a growing golden muzzle
   glow. The player is promised a fair read: the warning colour ramp *is* the
   contract. Never spawn a ball without the telegraph having played.

4. **Machined bronze, weathered wood.** Cannons read as real objects: a bronze
   barrel with a vertical light-to-dark gradient, a dark bore, brown wooden
   carriage and wheels, and a short recoil kick + smoke puff on fire. They are the
   one "manufactured" thing on an organic island — that is what makes them read as
   hostile hardware.

5. **The pirate is a friendly token, not a sprite sheet.** A small round striped
   body with a red bandana, rotated to face movement. Kept tiny and iconic so the
   balls dominate the frame; its charm is in the silhouette, not detail.

## Palette

- Sea: `#2bb7c4` → `#159bb3` → `#0c7ea0` (radial, lighter near the shore), white foam rings.
- Beach / wet shore: `#d9b26a`; dry sand `#f4dda3` → `#eccf86` → `#e2be6f`.
- Bronze cannon: `#f2c869` / `#c8912f` / `#8a5f1c`; wood `#6b3f1e` / `#4d2c13`.
- Cannonball: `#5a5a66` → `#26262e` → `#0a0a10` (near-black), white specular.
- Danger accent: amber `#ffb43c` → red `#d22828`.
- Pirate: cream `#f4f1e8` + red stripes `#d23b3b`, bandana `#c0392b`, skin `#e7b98a`.
- Accent / UI gold: `#f2b134`, cream text `#fff3d6`.

Everything answers to principle 1: if a new element is dark, round and on the
sand, it had better be a cannonball.
